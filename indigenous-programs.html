Indigenous programs

The CRA provides specific guidance and services for First Nations, Inuit, and Métis peoples. The primary framework is Section 87 of the Indian Act, which exempts from taxation the personal property of a status Indian situated on a reserve. Understanding when and how this exemption applies is essential for content designers working on Indigenous-related tax content.

Section 87 tax exemptions

Section 87 of the Indian Act provides that the personal property of an Indian or Indian band situated on a reserve is exempt from taxation. For income tax purposes, this means:

  • Employment income — exempt when duties are performed on a reserve or when connecting factors indicate the income is situated on a reserve
  • Investment income — exempt when the investment property is situated on a reserve
  • Business income — may be exempt depending on location and connecting factors
  • GST/HST relief — goods delivered to a reserve and services performed entirely on a reserve are relieved from GST/HST
  • Excise tax exemptions — for qualifying property on a reserve

The exemption applies to status Indians as defined under the Indian Act. It does not automatically apply to all Indigenous peoples.

Indian Act Exemption for Employment Income (IAEI)

The IAEI guidelines use a connecting factors test to determine whether employment income is situated on a reserve and therefore exempt. Factors include:

  • Where the duties of employment are performed
  • Where the employer resides (is the employer resident on a reserve?)
  • Where the employee resides
  • The nature of the work (is it for the benefit of Indians on a reserve?)

The guidelines provide specific scenarios and rules for common situations, including teachers, health care workers, and employees of Indian band councils.

Benefits access

Indigenous peoples are eligible for all standard CRA-administered benefits:

  • Canada Child Benefit (CCB)
  • GST/HST credit
  • Canada Workers Benefit (CWB)
  • Climate Action Incentive Payment (CAIP)
  • All other federal and provincial benefits administered by the CRA

Critical point: A T1 return must be filed to access these benefits, even if all income is exempt under Section 87. Filing the return triggers the benefit calculations; not filing means no benefits are paid regardless of eligibility.

Outreach and services

  • Community Volunteer Income Tax Program (CVITP) — provides free tax filing help in Indigenous communities
  • Outreach and liaison officers — dedicated CRA staff who work with Indigenous communities to increase benefits uptake and assist with filing
  • Tax information for Indigenous peoples in business — guidance on when business income is or is not exempt

Self-governing Indigenous groups

Some self-governing Indigenous groups have tax administration agreements with the federal government. These agreements may provide for:

  • Personal income tax on settlement lands
  • GST/HST on settlement lands
  • Tax rebate or remission arrangements

The CRA acts as a clearinghouse for tax information related to these agreements.
