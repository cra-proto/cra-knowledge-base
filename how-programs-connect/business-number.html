The Business Number foundation

The Business Number (BN) is the 9-digit root identifier for every business interaction with the CRA. It is the counterpart to the Social Insurance Number (SIN) for individuals.

How it branches

A single BN can have multiple program accounts. Each program account adds a 2-letter program identifier and a 4-digit reference number (e.g., 123456789 RT 0001).

Portals that use the BN

  • My Business Account — the business self-serve portal (equivalent of My Account for individuals)
  • Represent a Client — used by authorized representatives to act on behalf of businesses

Why this matters for content designers

When writing about any business program, the BN is the starting point. A business cannot register for GST/HST, open a payroll account, or file a corporate return without first having a BN. Content about any of these programs should reference the BN registration step.
