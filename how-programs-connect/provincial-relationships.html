Federal and provincial administration

The CRA does not administer all taxes in all provinces. Understanding which province handles what is important for content designers, especially when writing about provincial credits and corporate tax.

Individual income tax

The CRA administers provincial and territorial individual income tax for all provinces and territories except Quebec. Revenu Québec administers its own personal income tax. This means:

  • In all provinces except Quebec, individuals file one T1 return and the CRA calculates and collects both federal and provincial tax
  • In Quebec, individuals file two separate returns (federal T1 to CRA, provincial TP-1 to Revenu Québec)

Corporate income tax

The CRA administers provincial corporate income tax for all provinces except Quebec and Alberta, which have their own corporate tax administrations:

  • Quebec: corporations file a separate CO-17 return with Revenu Québec
  • Alberta: corporations file a separate AT1 return with Tax and Revenue Administration (TRA)
  • All other provinces: corporate tax is calculated on the T2 return and collected by the CRA

Provincial benefits through the T1

Even in provinces that administer their own taxes (like Quebec), some provincial benefits are still delivered through the federal T1 system. In all other provinces, provincial credits and benefits (like the Ontario Trillium Benefit or BC Climate Action Tax Credit) flow automatically from the T1 return.

Implications for content

When writing CRA content that references provincial tax:

  • Always specify whether a rule applies federally, provincially, or both
  • Be aware that Quebec residents have a fundamentally different filing experience
  • Alberta corporations have additional filing requirements beyond the T2
  • HST provinces have different rate structures than GST-only provinces
