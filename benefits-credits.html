Benefits and credits

The CRA administers dozens of federal and provincial benefit programs. All of them depend on the individual filing a T1 return — without a filed return, benefits are suspended regardless of eligibility. Net income and adjusted family net income are the primary inputs for benefit calculations.

Canada Child Benefit (CCB)

Monthly income-tested payment for eligible families with children under 18 years of age. Recalculated every July based on the prior year's T1 return.

  • Base CCB payment — amount decreases as adjusted family net income rises
  • Child disability benefit (CDB) — additional CCB supplement for children eligible for the disability tax credit
  • Provincial and territorial child benefits administered through CCB — Alberta, BC, New Brunswick, Newfoundland and Labrador, Northwest Territories, Nova Scotia, Nunavut, Ontario, PEI, Saskatchewan, Yukon (Quebec administers its own)

Key forms: RC66 (Canada Child Benefits Application), RC66SCH (Status in Canada)

GST/HST credit

Quarterly payments (July, October, January, April) for individuals and families with low and modest incomes. Eligibility is determined automatically from the T1 return.

  • Income-tested based on adjusted family net income
  • No separate application required — file a T1 return to be assessed
  • Provincial components where applicable

Note: The Canada Groceries and Essentials Benefit may evolve from or supplement this credit. See repos: canada-groceries-essentials-benefit---subway-2026, groceries-benefit-one-time-payment

Climate Action Incentive Payment (CAIP)

Quarterly payments for residents of provinces under the federal fuel charge. Includes a 10% rural supplement.

  • Provinces covered: Ontario, Manitoba, Saskatchewan, Alberta, New Brunswick, Nova Scotia, PEI, Newfoundland and Labrador
  • Determined from T1 return (Schedule 14)
  • Amounts vary by province and family size

Canada Workers Benefit (CWB)

Refundable tax credit for low-income workers, calculated directly on the T1 return.

  • Basic CWB amount — phases in on employment income, phases out at higher net income
  • CWB disability supplement — additional amount for DTC-eligible workers
  • Advance payments — receive up to 50% of estimated CWB in advance via Form RC201
  • Secondary earner exemption — reduces phase-out impact for two-income couples

Disability tax credit (DTC)

The DTC is both a non-refundable credit and a gateway that unlocks multiple other programs. Requires an approved Form T2201 (Disability Tax Credit Certificate) signed by a medical practitioner.

Programs unlocked by DTC approval

  • RDSP eligibility — cannot open a Registered Disability Savings Plan without DTC approval
  • Canada Disability Savings Grant and Bond — require an RDSP, which requires DTC
  • Child disability benefit (CDB) — additional CCB supplement
  • CWB disability supplement
  • Home accessibility tax credit (HATC)
  • Multigenerational home renovation tax credit
  • Qualified Disability Trust (QDT) status — beneficiary must be DTC-eligible

The DTC can be transferred to a supporting person if the individual cannot use the full credit amount.

Canadian Dental Care Plan

Originally introduced as the interim Canada Dental Benefit for children under 12, this has expanded into the broader Canadian Dental Care Plan (CDCP) providing dental coverage for eligible residents.

Provincial and territorial benefits

The CRA administers numerous provincial and territorial credits and benefits through the T1 return. These are automatically calculated when the return is filed.

Ontario

Ontario Trillium Benefit (OTB) — combines Ontario sales tax credit, energy and property tax credit, and Northern Ontario energy credit into a single monthly payment.

British Columbia

BC Climate Action Tax Credit — quarterly payment. BC family benefit and BC early childhood tax benefit delivered through CCB.

Atlantic provinces
  • New Brunswick — Harmonized Sales Tax Credit, child tax benefit, school supplement
  • Nova Scotia — Affordable Living Tax Credit, child benefit
  • PEI — Sales Tax Credit
  • Newfoundland and Labrador — Income Supplement, child benefit, mother baby nutrition supplement
Prairies and territories
  • Alberta — child and family benefit (through CCB)
  • Saskatchewan — Low-Income Tax Credit
  • Manitoba — various credits through T1
  • Northwest Territories — Cost of Living Offset, child benefit
  • Nunavut — Carbon Credit, child benefit
  • Yukon — Government Carbon Price Rebate, child benefit

Other benefits

  • Old Age Security (OAS) — primarily ESDC, but CRA calculates GIS based on T1 income
  • Guaranteed Income Supplement (GIS) — income-tested, requires T1 filing
  • Children's Special Allowances (CSA) — payments to agencies and institutions caring for children (repo: CSA)
  • Canada Carbon Rebate for Small Businesses — see Business section (repo: CCR-SB)
