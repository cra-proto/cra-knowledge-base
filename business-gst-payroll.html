Business, GST/HST and payroll

The Business Number (BN) is the 9-digit root identifier for all CRA business accounts. Every business interaction — GST/HST, payroll, corporate tax, charities, import/export — branches from the BN through program-specific account suffixes (RT, RP, RC, RR, RM, RD, RZ).

Business Number and program accounts

The BN is assigned when a business first registers with the CRA. Program accounts are added to the BN as needed:

GST/HST

The Goods and Services Tax / Harmonized Sales Tax is the CRA's largest business program by volume.

Registration and filing
  • Registration is mandatory when revenues exceed $30,000 in four consecutive calendar quarters or a single quarter
  • File GST/HST returns on Form GST34 — monthly, quarterly, or annually depending on revenue
  • Claim input tax credits (ITCs) to recover GST/HST paid on business expenses
  • Electronic filing via GST/HST NETFILE or GST/HST TELEFILE
  • Quick Method of Accounting available for small businesses (simplified calculation)
Special rules and sectors
  • Public service bodies' rebate — municipalities, hospitals, charities, schools
  • New housing rebate and new residential rental property rebate
  • Digital economy — non-resident digital platform operators must register and collect
  • Financial institutions — Selected Listed Financial Institutions (SLFI) have separate rules
  • Zero-rated and exempt supplies — basic groceries, prescription drugs, medical devices are zero-rated; financial services, residential rent are exempt

Payroll

Employers must register for a payroll account (RP) and handle source deductions:

  • Source deductions: CPP contributions, EI premiums, income tax withholding
  • T4 Summary and slips — annual reporting of employment income
  • T4A Summary and slips — pension, retirement, annuity, and other income
  • Remittance schedules — regular, threshold 1, threshold 2, or quarterly depending on average monthly withholding amount
  • Payroll Deductions Online Calculator (PDOC) — tool for calculating deductions
  • Taxable benefits — automobile, parking, group insurance, stock options require complex calculations

Key guides: T4001 (Employers' Guide), T4130 (Taxable Benefits and Allowances)

SR&ED (Scientific Research and Experimental Development)

The SR&ED program provides investment tax credits (ITCs) for qualifying research and development activities.

  • Enhanced rate for CCPCs: 35% refundable credit on first $3 million of qualifying expenditures
  • General rate: 15% non-refundable credit for other claimants
  • Form T661 — SR&ED Expenditures Claim (describes work and expenditures)
  • Schedule T2SCH31 — Investment Tax Credit for corporations
  • Form T2038(IND) — Investment Tax Credit for individuals
  • First-Time Claimant Advisory Service and pre-claim consultation available

Excise taxes and duties

  • Excise duties (Excise Act, 2001) — specific duties on spirits, wine, beer, cannabis, tobacco, vaping products
  • Excise taxes (Excise Tax Act, Parts other than Part IX) — taxes on specific goods such as automobile air conditioners, fuel-inefficient vehicles, insurance premiums; distinct from excise duties
  • Air Travellers Security Charge
  • Fuel charge — under the Greenhouse Gas Pollution Pricing Act
  • Luxury tax — on certain vehicles, aircraft, and vessels over price thresholds (Select Luxury Items Tax Act)
  • Customs tariff — duties on goods imported into Canada; administered in coordination with CBSA
  • Cannabis duty and vaping product duty — reported on specialized forms (B300, B301, B601)

Digital Services Tax (DST)

A 3% tax on Canadian-source digital services revenue for large businesses (consolidated group revenue of 750 million EUR or more globally, with $20 million or more in Canadian digital services revenue). Effective January 1, 2022 (enacted retroactively).

Form: DST1

Related repos: dst, digital-services-tax

Clean economy investment tax credits

A suite of new ITCs introduced to encourage clean energy investment:

  • Carbon Capture, Utilization, and Storage (CCUS) ITC
  • Clean Technology ITC
  • Clean Hydrogen ITC
  • Clean Electricity ITC
