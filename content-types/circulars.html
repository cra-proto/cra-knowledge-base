Information circulars

Information Circulars (ICs) describe the CRA's administrative procedures, policies, and guidelines. They focus on how the CRA operates rather than on interpreting the law.

What they cover

ICs explain CRA processes that taxpayers and practitioners interact with — how to request a ruling, what happens during an audit, how the VDP works, how long to keep records.

How ICs relate to other content

ICs complement folios (which interpret the law) and guides (which help taxpayers complete returns). ICs are directed at both taxpayers and CRA staff, explaining the mechanics of CRA processes.

Think of the relationship this way:

  • Folio: "Here is what the law means"
  • IC: "Here is how we administer that law"
  • Guide: "Here is how you, the taxpayer, comply with that law"
  • Form: "Here is the document you fill out to comply"
