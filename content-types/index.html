CRA content types

The CRA publishes content through distinct publication types, each serving a different purpose and carrying a different level of authority. Understanding these types helps content designers know what they are creating, what sources to reference, and where their work fits in the larger information architecture.

Sections

Forms
Official CRA documents that taxpayers fill out to provide information, make claims, or fulfill obligations
Guides and pamphlets
Comprehensive how-to guides and short plain-language pamphlets for specific tax topics
Folios and interpretation bulletins
The CRA's detailed interpretive positions on the Income Tax Act, organized by series and chapter
Rulings and technical interpretations
Binding advance rulings and non-binding technical interpretations for specific transactions
Information circulars
CRA publications describing administrative procedures and how the CRA operates
Campaigns and news releases
Seasonal awareness campaigns and official CRA announcements
The authority hierarchy
How all CRA content types relate to each other, from legislation to campaigns
