Guides and pamphlets

Guides and pamphlets are both explanatory publications, but they serve different audiences and levels of detail.

Guides

Guides are comprehensive reference documents that walk taxpayers through how to complete a specific return or navigate a complex tax situation. They are numbered, versioned, and often available as both PDF and HTML.

Key characteristics

  • Longer and more structured than regular web pages
  • Designed to be read as a complete reference for a topic
  • Each guide has a unique identifier (T4001, RC4022, etc.)
  • Updated annually or as legislation changes

Pamphlets

Pamphlets are shorter, more accessible publications that provide a general overview of a topic. They introduce concepts and direct readers to more detailed resources.

Key characteristics

  • Shorter and less technical than guides
  • Written for a general audience in plain language
  • Often used for outreach and public education

Important pamphlets

  • P102 — Support Payments
  • P105 — Students and Income Tax
  • P113 — Gifts and Income Tax
  • P119 — When You Retire
  • P148 — Resolving Your Dispute: Objection and Appeal Rights
  • RC4070 — Information for Canadian Small Businesses

Pamphlet P105
