Forms

Forms are the primary mechanism through which taxpayers interact with CRA programs. A form is a fillable document with a unique alphanumeric identifier — it collects data, it does not explain.

How forms relate to other content

A form implements a program. A guide explains how to fill out the form. A folio explains the CRA's interpretation of the law behind the form. An information circular explains how the CRA administers the process. Think of forms as the operational layer — everything else either interprets the law the form implements or helps the taxpayer complete the form correctly.

Example chain:

  • Form T2201 (DTC application) is explained by Guide RC4064 (Disability-Related Information), interpreted by Folio S1-F1-C2 (Disability Tax Credit), and the CRA's administration of DTC claims is described in information circulars
