Rulings and technical interpretations

The CRA's Income Tax Rulings Directorate provides two types of written responses: advance income tax rulings (binding) and technical interpretations (non-binding).

Advance income tax rulings

A ruling is a binding written statement by the CRA on how it will apply specific provisions of the Income Tax Act to a proposed transaction.

  • Requested by: taxpayers or their representatives before a transaction occurs
  • Binding: yes — the CRA is bound by the ruling as long as the facts match the proposal
  • Fee-based: starting at $300, depending on complexity
  • Published: redacted versions are available for public reference
  • Governed by: Information Circular IC70-6

Technical interpretations

A technical interpretation is a written response to a specific technical question about how the Income Tax Act applies to a particular fact pattern.

  • Requested by: taxpayers, representatives, or CRA staff
  • Binding: no — they represent the CRA's view but cannot be relied upon as a guarantee
  • Published: redacted versions are published on the CRA website
  • Useful for: guidance when folios and guides do not address a specific scenario

Key difference

Rulings address proposed transactions and are binding. Technical interpretations can address past, current, or hypothetical scenarios and are non-binding. Both are more specific than folios, which state general principles.
