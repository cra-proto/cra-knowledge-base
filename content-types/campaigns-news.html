Campaigns and news releases

Campaigns and news releases are the CRA's outward-facing communication tools. Unlike reference content, they are designed to persuade, inform about changes, and drive action.

Campaign pages

Campaign pages are promotional or informational web pages tied to specific CRA initiatives, awareness drives, or seasonal deadlines.

Common campaign topics

  • Tax filing season ("It's tax time" — filing deadlines, tips, free services)
  • Benefits awareness ("You may be eligible" — reaching people who are not claiming benefits they qualify for)
  • Fraud and scam awareness ("Protect yourself" — recognizing fake CRA calls, emails, texts)
  • Free tax clinic promotion (CVITP — Community Volunteer Income Tax Program)
  • New program announcements (dental benefit, FHSA, etc.)
  • Digital services promotion (My Account, auto-fill my return, NETFILE)

How they differ from regular pages

Campaign pages are often temporary or seasonal, use a more persuasive tone, and are designed around a communications objective rather than a reference need. Regular CRA pages are evergreen reference content. Campaign pages may use the GCWeb campaign template, which allows for more flexible layouts.

News releases

Official CRA and Department of Finance communications announcing new policies, enforcement actions, administrative updates, and program changes. Published on the Canada.ca newsroom.

Types

  • Policy and program announcements
  • Budget implementation updates
  • Tax conviction announcements (public naming of convicted tax evaders)
  • Benefit payment date announcements
  • Scam warnings and advisories
  • Statistical releases (e.g., tax gap reports)
  • Statements by the Minister of National Revenue

"Read me" pages

Brief introductory or orientation pages that serve as landing points before users go deeper into a topic. They answer "What is this about?" and "Where should I go?" — functioning as navigational hubs or tables of contents. These are not a formal content type but are a common pattern on the CRA website.
